Why is the requirements gathering stage important?

     In software development, the requirement gathering stage is important to have a clear and concise description of the set of operations the software is intended to perform based on the client's needs. To have successful software, we should ask the right questions, such as: What does the client need the software to do? Who is using the software? How the user will interact with the software? Lastly, what contraints do we have? These questions lead us to our two main types of requirements, functional and non-functional. Functional requirements describe what the software should do. Non-functional requirements encompass the manner the system will perform, the limitations of the software, the specific type of data it will handle, the demographics of its users and any environmental factors (such as geographical legislations). As we gather our requirements, they should be documented in a checklist so that everyone is on the same page of the expectations. The requirements should be clearly numbered, described with simple language, prioritized and include any dependencies with it. With a well-defined checklist, we can prevent project delays by reducing unnecessary costs, accumulation of redundant requirements, or ineffective solutions.
