Why is the requirements gathering stage important?

     In software development, the requirement gathering stage is important to have a clear and concise description of the set of operations the software is intended to perform based on the client's needs. To have a succesful software, we should ask the right questions, such as, what the clients needs the software to do? Who is using the software? How the users will interact with the software? Lastly, what contraints do we have? These questions will lead us to our two main types of requirements, functional and non-functional. Functional requirements describe what the software should do. Non-functional requirements encompass the manner the system will perform, the limitations of the software, the specific type of data it will be handling, the demographic of its users and any environmental factors (such as geographical legislations). Some methods of requirements gathering include brainstorming, surveys, interviews and user feedback from the stakeholders and team(s) involved with the software. As we gather our requirements, it should be displayed on a document checklist so that everyone is on the same page of the expectations. The requirements should be clearly numbered, described with simple language, prioritized and include any dependencies with it. With a well-defined checklist, we can prevent project delays by reducing unnecessary cost, accumulation of redundant requirements, or ineffective solutions.
